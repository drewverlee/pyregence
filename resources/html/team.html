About Us

Pyregence consortium is founded on open science and technology principles, employing inclusivity and collaboration to tackle complex challenges.

Consortium partners are from leading research institutions from industry, academia, and government.

Workgroups

Workgroup #2

Workgroup #3

Workgroup #4

Integration Team

Advising and facilitating the integration of findings and products across project workgroups and externally with other research initiatives.

Jason Moghaddas, M.Sc., RPF

Operation Coordinator

Spatial Informatics Group

Jason is responsible for administrative coordination of the project. He will work closely with the Project Manager, Principal Investigator and Project Attorney to ensure the contracting arrangement for the project is in place at project inception. During the project he will support the Project Manager on reporting and invoicing. Ensures that accounts payable are paid in a timely manner. Jason received both his BS and MS from UC Berkeley, with his studies and research focused on fire and riparian ecology. He brings over 15 years of experience in natural resource project planning and management to SIG. Jason has been a lead or co-author on over 20 published scientific papers. In addition to a strong research background, He brings the practical experience of being a current Registered Professional Forest (#2774) along with extensive experience in the Sierras as a wildland firefighter and wildlife field technician. Through his work as a Fire Ecologist on the Plumas National Forest, Mr. Moghaddas worked as part of an US Forest Service interdisciplinary team to plan and conduct National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) analysis for landscape level fuel treatments in a politically charged climate. As Conservation Director for the Feather River Land Trust, Mr. Moghaddas worked with large landowners to help develop and fund conservation easements on working ranches in Plumas County.

Shane Romsos, M.Sc.

Project Manager

Spatial Informatics Group

Shane is responsible for managing day-to-day tasks related to the project and reporting to CEC and the Principal Investigator. Shane is a wildlife biologist with more than 25 years of professional natural resources management, policy and research experience in the public, private and non-profit sectors. Prior to joining SIG, he was the Science Program Manager and Wildlife and Fisheries Program Manager for the Tahoe Regional Planning Agency, and was on the Forest Plan Revision Team for the US Forest Service. In these roles, he was responsible for overseeing staff and planning and management of annual budgets, the design and implementation of a regional-scale multi-indicator status and trend monitoring and evaluation program, leading regional planning efforts for natural resource conservation, contributing to a science plan for the Tahoe Region (including prioritizing and distributing science grant funds), preparing and implementing natural resources and socio-economic monitoring plans, and conducting GIS and remote sensing analysis. At SIG, he has been involved in several research projects, including serving as the Project Manager for the CPUC Wildfire Threat Map 2, CALFIRE Life Cycle Assessment for Forest Practice Rules, ACR Improved Forest Management carbon projects, Stream Environment Zone and Fisheries Habitat Mapping projects and others. He has a BS in Wildlife and a M.Sc. in Natural Resources from Humboldt State University.

David Saah, PhD

Lead Project Investigator

Spatial Informatics Group and University of San Francisco

Project Principal Investigator, Spatial Informatics Group - Dr. Saah has the responsibility for oversight of the project. Dr. Saah has been broadly trained as an environmental scientist with expertise in a number of areas including: landscape ecology, ecosystem ecology, hydrology, geomorphology, ecosystem modeling, natural hazard modeling, remote sensing, geographic information systems (GIS) and geospatial analysis. He has used these skills to conduct research primarily at the landscape level in a variety of systems. Dr. Saah has participated in research projects throughout the United States and Internationally. His academic research uses integrated geospatial science for multi-scale mapping, monitoring and modeling of environmental spatial heterogeneity, particularly in riparian, savanna, and forest ecosystems. These efforts include quantification of change in landscape pattern, investigating the linkages between pattern and processes, and understanding the pattern-process dynamic within different environmental management regimes. To complement this, Dr. Saah’s consulting research interest and experience include: developing holistic decision support systems for resource management, assessing natural hazards, and quantifying ecosystem service valuation. In addition, all of his research addresses access, availability, and accuracy of geospatial and environmental datasets, and scale in natural resource and environmental research. Dr. Saah is committed to producing high quality research projects that integrate the most current science and technology. He is dedicated to the accurate dissemination of results from these endeavors through innovative presentations, publications, and workshops.
