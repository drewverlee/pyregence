Forecast Tools

Forecasting wildfire activity in California to inform the operation of a safe and reliable electric grid

Situational awareness of hazardous fire conditions and the potential impacts of wildfire, both to and from the electric grid, is vital to maintain infrastructure integrity. Electric utilities rely on timely and actionable intel to mitigate fire risk, yet there are numerous barriers to accessing information: current wildfire science and technology is fragmented and limited, and software is either unable to function at scale or hidden in a black box behind a paywall.

The Pyregence Consortium provides free and open source access to software tools and data backed by a fire and risk forecasting system specifically developed for use by Californiaâ€™s electric utilities. A web-based, interactive interface displays forecasts for active fires, fire weather, and fire risk at a seven-day horizon. A user-selected output data structure allows for flexible integration with existing workflows to assist decision-making for fire and ignition risk mitigation, thus reducing losses and enhancing grid safety and reliability.

Pyregence Tools

Electric utilities currently use fire models that are based on dated wildfire science. PRC is providing free and open source access to cutting-edge wildfire science and tools that provide for timely and accurate forecasts of wildfire spread and potential impacts to the electric utility grid and communities.

Goals

Develop the next generation of models to provide near-term risk forecasts at a 0-to-7-day temporal scale and at a fine (circa 30m) spatial scale to simulate spread of extant fire ignitions and enhance current models with best practice in wildfire risk modeling.

Integrate a decision-support tool into operating procedures at IOUs.

High Impact Products

Web-platform to view active fire forecasts, 0 to 7 day fire probability and de-energization metrics from multiple wildfire models

Enhancement of current wildfire models (Elmfire/GridFire) to include fire in the WUI, coupled fire-weather dynamics, spotting, amongst others

Future re-design of wildfire modelling to incorporate new developments in basic science of fire behaviour modelling
