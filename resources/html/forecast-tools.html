Forecast Tools

Forecasting wildfire activity in California and beyond

During wildfires, situational awareness is vital to maintain electric utility infrastructure. The Pyregence Project will provide free and open source access to data, model outputs and web-based risk forecasting tools that are based on the state of wildfire science. This fire activity and risk forecasting system has been in development over the last decade and through the Pyregence Project will be made available to wildfire practitioners. The Pyregence web-based, interactive interface displays forecasts for active fires and fire risk at a five-day horizon. Pyregence Tools

Electric utilities currently use fire models that are based on dated wildfire science. PRC is providing free and open source access to cutting-edge wildfire science and tools that provide for timely and accurate forecasts of wildfire spread and potential impacts to the electric utility grid and communities.

Goals

Develop the next generation of models to provide near-term risk forecasts at a 0-to-7-day temporal scale and at a fine (circa 30m) spatial scale to simulate spread of extant fire ignitions and enhance current models with best practice in wildfire risk modeling.

Integrate a decision-support tool into operating procedures at IOUs.

High Impact Products

Web-platform to view active fire forecasts, 5 day fire probability and de-energization metrics from multiple wildfire models

Enhancement of current wildfire models (Elmfire/GridFire) to include fire in the WUI, coupled fire-weather dynamics, suppression, amongst others

Future re-design of wildfire modelling to incorporate new developments in basic science of fire behaviour modelling
